About Depression

Depression is a serious and common medical psychological condition that affects the way you think, feel, and act. It interferes with your abilities and makes it difficult to do your everyday tasks.

Many things influence it, Changes in the brain, like chemical imbalances, can contribute Hormones and genetics also play a part. Depression is not just one thingâ€” it's a mix of factors from our bodies, minds, and lives. Understanding all these parts helps us understand and treat depression better.

Test 1: Rating Test

This test uses a numerical scale to assess the severity of depression symptoms based on predefined questions about mood, energy, sleep, and interest in activities. It provides a quick and quantifiable overview of the user's mental health.

Test 2: Questionnaire

Open-ended questions are analyzed using natural language processing (NLP) to detect patterns, emotions, and keywords indicative of depression. This approach offers deeper insights by examining the user's expressive thoughts and feelings.

Test 3: Thematic Apperception Test (TAT)

Users interpret ambiguous images or tell stories about them, revealing subconscious emotions and thought patterns. It helps uncover hidden concerns or unresolved conflicts, offering a unique perspective on mental well-being.
